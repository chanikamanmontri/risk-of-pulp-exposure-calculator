**Risk of Pulp Exposure Prediction Calculator**

A web-based clinical decision support tool that estimates the probability of pulp exposure during non-selective caries removal in pediatric permanent molars with extensive caries. The calculator implements a multivariable logistic regression model developed and validated at the Faculty of Dentistry, Chiang Mai University, Thailand.

The model incorporates eight preoperative clinical and radiographic predictors: patient age, sex, dental arch (maxillary/mandibular), carious site involvement, pulpal diagnosis (normal pulp, reversible pulpitis, or irreversible pulpitis), radiographic caries depth (RC5 or RC6), root development status, and presence of early periapical changes. Based on the predicted probability, the tool categorizes risk into three levels — low (<30%), moderate (30–70%), and high (>70%) — and provides corresponding clinical recommendations for material preparation, informed consent, and vital pulp therapy planning.

The prediction model was developed using a retrospective cohort of pediatric patients who underwent non-selective caries removal on permanent molars, with internal validation using bootstrap resampling and temporal external validation in a separate cohort. Model coefficients were recalibrated to optimize performance in the validation dataset.

This tool is intended for use by dental practitioners, pediatric dentists, and dental students as a preoperative planning aid. It does not prescribe treatment selection; clinical decisions should be guided by intraoperative findings, pulp tissue status, and established operative principles.

Reference: Manmontri C, et al. Clinical Prediction Model for Pulp Exposure during Non-Selective Caries Removal in Pediatric Permanent Molars with Extensive Caries: Development and Validation.
